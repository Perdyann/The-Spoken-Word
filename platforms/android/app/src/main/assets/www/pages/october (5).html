5. BLESSED IN A HARD PLACE

And he lighted upon a certain place, and tarried there all night, because the sun was set; and he took of the stones of that place, and put them for his pillows, and lay down in that place to sleep.

And, behold, the LORD stood above it, and said, I am the LORD God of Abraham thy father, and the God of Isaac: the land whereon thou liest, to thee will I give it, and to thy seed; (Genesis 28:11 & 13)

Even in the most hopeless places, God can bring forth an unexpected miracle.

After Jacob had departed from Beersheba, he journeyed towards Haran. There was a certain place, where he had to spend the night in, because the sun was already set.

That land, where he tarried was not his comfort zone; it was a place characterized by its level of malaise and incommode. This was to the extent that the only pillows Jacob could lay his head on were stones.

Have you ever been through such hardness where stones were the only pillows you had?

I recall several instances of sleeping on hard pillows; this only resulted in neck-pain and nightmares. If a genuine pillow that was merely hard could result in neck-pain, how much more pain would you feel when your head is laid upon real stones?

This defines the level of inconvenience Jacob had to tolerate for the night; in such a place where hardness was portrayed in every way, blessings were birthed.

Rather than having nightmares, as I would have anticipated, Jacob saw a great vision in his sleep. It was in that same hard place that God began to spell out blessings upon him and his seed.

In that same hard place, God released the blessing of possession, multiplication and preservation. Isnâ€™t it amazing how God releases blessings out of the places we least expect?

Of all places God could bless Jacob in, He chose a hard place; of all places God could take Ezekiel to, He chose the valley of dry bones. Of all places God chose to raise Lazarus from, it was the tomb.

Jesus could have healed Lazarus while he was sick, but He tarried till he had rested in the tomb for days; the Spirit of God could have led Ezekiel to a hospital to heal the sick, but he led him to valley of dry bones to give life to that, which was already considered hopeless.

Even in the most hopeless places, God can bring forth an unexpected miracle. No matter how inconvenient and uncomfortable the place, where you are seems; no matter how hopeless the situation seems; always bear in mind that there is no place too hard or hopeless for a miracle to occur.

Trust God in your situation; even when the only pillows you can find is the stones, still trust God for a blessed vision. The place might be hard to dwell in, but it is not too hard to contain a blessing. Be blessed!
