11. THE CHURCH: TOGETHERNESS

All the believers were together and had everything in common. (Acts 2:44)

Breaking a single stick of broom is much easier than breaking a pile of broomsticks bonded together.

There is a common lesson of togetherness, often portrayed using broomsticks. It is said, breaking a single stick of broom is much easier than breaking a pile of broomsticks bonded together.

When a stick is on its own, it can easily be bent, crushed and destroyed, but when it is in the company of other sticks, it is fairly infeasible to destroy it.

Just like the broomsticks, any nation that is divided against itself cannot stand; any organization or body that does not share the same commonalities, goals and are not blended in unity can easily be accessed and destroyed.

Little wonder it is said, “united we stand, divided we fall.” When a body is united in love, sold out to God, and founded on the Word of God, it cannot but stand firm against all odds. It is written:

And the eye cannot say unto the hand, I have no need of thee: nor again the head to the feet, I have no need of you. (1 Corinthians 12:21)

Different members work together, for the human body to function properly. What the eye does, the hands cannot do; but when all parts work together, nothing remains undone.

Likewise, not one ministry has the entirety of God. What makes a church is the ability for different members to work together, for the body to function properly. Without togetherness, there is no body and no church.

If there’s one quality that shouldn’t be lacking in any Church, it is togetherness. The believers of the early Church remained conscious of this; they had everything in common, and stayed together always.

Togetherness does not necessarily imply being physically present with someone, but being spiritually connected to everyone. They might not have seen everyone on a daily basis, but made efforts to pray for them, and show concern at all seasons.

Beloved, how connected are you to your brethren? How available are you to meet the needs of others? How concerned are you about your neighbors?

Just like the human body works, when one part is injured, the hurt is felt everywhere. When a single toe is amputated, the whole body feels the pain, because there is a connection.

Rejoice with those who rejoice, share with those who need, be a blessing to everyone. We are united by one God, one blood, and one Word. Be blessed!
