22. RUN TO OBTAIN

Know ye not that they which run in a race run all, but one receiveth the prize? So run, that ye may obtain. (1 Corinthians 9:24)

Difference is what defines the winner of a race; you must not run on the track of everyone else; you must not follow the pace of everyone else.

Great men don’t just run a race, in order to be recognized as mere participants; they run intently, with a motive and goal of winning, and obtaining the prize.

Don’t just run for the pleasure and fun of it; don’t just run for a sense of belonging; don’t just run for that recognition as an athlete. Run with a focus and goal of being the best; start the race with a target of outrunning the other contestants.

Many athletes contest in a race; each of them have different motives and principles. What defines the winner is a predefined target, with embedded means of obtaining the crown.

Anything worth doing is worth doing well; if a race is worth running, it is worth running well. Life is a race; if it is worth living, then it is worth living well. It is a waste of time and effort, running a race, without having a victorious intention.

The scripture today admonishes us to run, that we may obtain. Although many contestants are involved in the race, we must run in a way that makes us outstanding.

For a single winner to be chosen out of a multitude of athletes, that winner must have done something that the crowd never thought of doing.

Great men are not those, who merely followed the crowd, but those, who have proven to be outstanding. Don’t try to be an imitation of everyone else; don’t make it a compulsion to follow the path of everyone else. Stick to the direction of the Holy Spirit, even when it seems like a weird path.

Be different from the world, have clearly defined goals and target. Be outstanding!

You can be outstanding irrespective of your career; many politicians were corrupt, but at least one stood out; many professionals were treacherous, but at least one stood out. Choose to be the one that will stand out, even when everyone else is compromising.

Amongst the crowd that stood in the front of a displayed idol; Shedrach, Meshach and Abednego fetched themselves recognition and promotion, because they stood out, even when everyone else was ignorantly bowing.

Difference is what defines the winner of a race; you must not run on the track of everyone else; you must not follow the pace of everyone else; only run that ye may obtain. Be outstanding!
