13. HEALING OR WHOLENESS

And one of them, when he saw that he was healed, turned back, and with a loud voice glorified God,

And fell down on his face at his feet, giving him thanks: and he was a Samaritan.

And Jesus answering said, Were there not ten cleansed? but where are the nine?

There are not found that returned to give glory to God, save this stranger.

And he said unto him, Arise, go thy way: thy faith hath made thee whole. (Luke 17:15-19)

Wholeness is the best life-insurance policy you could ever opt for. More than God wants the sick healed, he wants them whole.

They were ten lepers, whom God healed; of these very numbers, only one turned back in thanksgiving and glorified God. As a result, he left with something that all others did not receive—wholeness.

Others were healed but not whole, whereas, this fellow, by faith received wholeness as well.

Which is more profitable: Healing or wholeness? Healing restores the physical health alone, but wholeness covers everything. Wholeness is defined as the unbroken completeness of everything, with nothing wanting.

What is left out of everything? Nothing! In essence, nothing can be left wanting, to a man of wholeness. By this, we can conclude that healing is a subset of wholeness.

In essence, by being physically healed, your body is ok, but by being whole, you are made complete in everything—health, prosperity, Wisdom, knowledge, understanding—with nothing wanting.

More than I desire to see the sick receive healing in their bodies, I desire wholeness. Wholeness is assurance that they will never be sick again; wholeness is totality! In essence, by being whole, their life is insured. Wholeness is not just bodily, but spiritual; that is, being equipped in spirit.

The nine lepers received healing in their bodies, and that was all; they’d probably remain spiritual liabilities and financially wretched. But by faith, in thanksgiving, the other leper insured his life by receiving wholeness.

The nine lepers were satisfied with healing; they were too used to the average life; they were comfortable with mediocrity. However, the grateful leper was a man of faith, and his faith fetched him something better than average.

Not many of us have acted differently from the nine lepers. We’ve prayed, and received our break-through, but often forget to be thankful.

Beloved, there is something greater than your previously anticipated break-through, which God still has in store for you. In order for you to receive greater, you have to be thankful.

Don’t be satisfied with healing, go for wholeness. Wholeness is the best life-insurance policy you could ever opt for. More than God wants the sick healed, he wants them whole.

Finally, it is important to understand that our attitude greatly influences what we can receive. With an attitude of gratitude, we can receive something more than what we previously received.

Character has limited many from reaching the heights prepared for them. Build your character in God’s Word! Character could release something more than healing — wholeness — Be blessed!
