27. THE VICTORY THAT OVERCOMES

For whatsoever is born of God overcometh the world: and this is the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith. (1 John 5:4)

The magnitude of your faith determines the extent of your victory.

There is something interesting about this scripture; firstly, we are given an assurance that whatsoever is born of God overcometh the world. Just as we begin to rejoice over that, the victory is pronounced.

It states, “ ... this is the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith.” Did you notice that faith is never described as ‘a victory’ that overcomes, but ‘the victory’ that overcomes?

If faith was indeed a victory that overcame the world, we would easily assume that there are other victories; but by the use of the term ‘the victory’, there is no room for assumptions. Faith is the only victory that overcomes; nothing more, nothing less!

Just the same way the scripture states that Jesus is the way, and not a way, because He is the only way; faith is the victory and not a victory, because it is the only victory. Faith is the victory that overcomes the world—without faith, the world can never be overcome.

Now, let us go back to the first part of that scripture: if indeed faith is the only victory that overcometh the world, why then are we assured that whatsoever is born of God overcomes?

Verily, whatsoever is born of God has faith already! The only issue is that there are different measures of faith, and we are expected to grow in faith. What a giant faith can do, a grain faith cannot do.

When Jesus told His disciples that a mustard-seed faith can move mountains, He was not referring to the initial size of the mustard seed, but the growing ability of the seed. In essence, a growing faith, and not a small faith is what causes great exploits.

People with growing and developed faith can move mountains, but those, who are stagnant in faith, can barely shift stones.

I have seen born-again men suffer like ordinary men; I have seen tongue-talking believers languish in pain like mere children of poverty stricken men. Why? They are probably stagnant in faith.

Even though by virtue of our new-birth, we should be victorious; this cannot be the case, when faith is not put to work. Verily, the magnitude of your faith is what determines the extent of your victory.

Beloved, faith is the victory that overcomes; you might be born again, but as long as you walk in fear, and not faith, then you cannot be victorious.

It is good to know that we have overcome already, and we are victorious already; but it is greater to know that all that we are in Christ is only activated by faith. Faith is the victory that overcomes — Be blessed!
